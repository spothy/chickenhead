Homely thoughts

Bon Iver - Sh'Diah

I'm inside the plane, and this old lady is sitting on my seat. I chose to sit by the window. Even more, I did everything that was on my hands to make sure I could stare into the sky during my 10-hour flight. I can't remember properly, but in my mind, I think I asked her in a very chivalrous way to move her ass away from my seat.

As lame as it may sound, I've always been fascinated by clouds. The way they expand towards the horizon, the patterns, the white shadows, and the bluish contrast. It's almost painful that something so majestic vanishes as soon as you touch it. As soon as it becomes real. I don't trust my eyes nor my mind. I see as far as my mind lets me, and I think everything is as I see it. My heart is alright, tho. If only I could close my eyes and become as ethereal as an Atlantic cloud. Expand, break, stick, flow, transform, bounce, disappear, and kidnap the sun during kid's birthday parties. I would see everything from a distance, a cloud with a soul, holding secrets, feelings, and anxiety. A decent cloud that would do good as well; I would be a gloomy afternoon for the ones in need of comfort; a feisty rain for the lovers on the street. I would stalk a lot of people. Like I would eavesdrop the shit out of the people that I love. I'd do it as they step on me as a puddle and on my way up to being a cloud after a rainy summer day. I would feed out of their tears and make myself heard during their awkward silences. I would be all of that and still feel very sad. I would be a cloud craving to be a human just to be seen.

I think the seat thief on my right is not impressed with my writing. So if you are reading this, Salvadorean lady, know that I'm hangover and a little bit heartbroken.

I hate how we speak of time as if we actually knew shit about it. Like how can three weeks fit in a second, and 15 days can feel like a kick in the cunt. Time goes and never returns, used to say, my 8th-grade teacher. A lovely lady, that flew all the way from the UK to spend her adulthood surrounded by Latino rascals. She got her hip replaced when she was 50, and every day, it took her 30 minutes to reach our classroom after the recess. She had green eyes, and her heart was as big as a watermelon. A sweet deep voice and an overwhelming appreciation for life. She lost her husband at a young age, and sometimes she would take some minutes to ramble about her lost love. Time never seemed to bother her. I remember, everyday she wore the emeralds bracelets that her dear bought for her. She said time goes and never returns, and still, she had golden seconds hanging out of her wrist.

Imagine life without the concept of time. Think about prisoners getting their freedom back, only if they've truly changed. A world ready for eternal love and ageless romance. It sounds very impractical, to be honest, and a free-for-all playground for sexual predators.

We all pretend to know what abstract concepts are and what they are not. Maybe it's relative, and in reality, we all are philosophers. Or perhaps not, and we all know that other's are dumber than we are, and we just can't be bother to give a shit about other people's idiocy. We can't change people's reality. For instance, I'm sure nobody in this plane can prove me wrong when I say that right now, while I'm sitting next to the window, playing solitaire, I'm also in the last Thursday morning, underneath my bedsheets, feeling blessed next to my sweetheart. I'm mother fucking certain that nobody from this 200 people crowd can deny that while I'm typing these words, I'm also in next weekend lousy Guatemala waiting for my loved one to reply to this that I'm writing now. I mean, they can try, they can say shit to me, they can touch me and therefore bring me back to the stupid 48A seat, but I only need one moment to diverge and be far away, holding my partner's hand while drinking wine on a Stockholm summer evening. As a last resource, they can join forces, and decide to take turns to poke me with a stick so that I could only be where they can see me. Or perhaps, they can prove me wrong and I'm an idiot pretending to have deep thoughts on a transatlantic flight. Maybe I am, and I'm not, maybe I'm infinite like the clouds outside of my window.

Where I go to, my lovely

Silence evacuates me. It rips me out of myself, and pushes me hard, like a child, into a river of violent thoughts. These are the thoughts that deconstruct and destroy you. The ones that take whatever bricks you’ve worked so hard at laying recently and cast them over their shoulders like wishing salts.

Silence takes the stories I tell about me. It drags the tongue out from inside of me and nails it to the closest door. The door you would walk through if I wasn’t stuck there hanging from it like some bad omen. Or old wives tale, discouraging wayward sailors from sailing too close to the rocks. Stories are all I have for you. Please don’t take them away.

My head is full with stories, and anecdotes, and moments – these revelatory, censored moments that I need in order to relay to you my ‘goodness’. In these snapshots I hand out of myself, I must always appear as ‘good’ as the story I’m spinning. I want to be witnessed by the pictures I take, the best angles only please. But what is goodness anyway? Does it come from the word ‘god’? And if god is a witness and you are mine(?), then – honestly – I am truly fucking grateful. Because you are more than some new fiction I tell myself. You make me feel goodness. You make me feel.

I looked it up, this word, ‘goodness’, and it seems to have nothing to do with god. I’m sort of glad, because god permeates our language like red wine on white t-shirts. And god is the purest most difficult kind of stain to wash out. God has chased me my whole life. God’s goodness has evaded me my whole life. I am a circle in this way. Both times I prayed, it ruined my clothes. God makes me naked. God makes me sad.

But while goodness is not godliness, (etymologically) it has almost everything to do with ‘consistency’, with being ‘fixed’, or ‘together’. All things I am emphatically not.

I’ve always felt like fragments. Like many lies stitched together, creating this patchwork of a person, with this grey, dull monster, writhing contemptuously underneath. Maybe this is why I struggle with conflict. Maybe this is why I cry. This river in me rages and I can’t let it out. If anyone saw it, they’d witness it, and leave. Or say something like, ‘fuck that girl is crazy’, and there would be no hope for me, and no pretty pictures or stories. If I lose my shit, they take my story. And I cannot bear it. Don’t take my goodness, please.

I don’t believe in god, in the Christian sense. Or the spiritual sense, as a matter of fact. There’s no denomination that explains our nature without blind spots. Because these gaping fucking wounds in the side of ‘His’ tortured chest tell you what? We still don’t know ourselves any better than the earth knows us. And if the earth knew any better it’d buck us off its back like wild horses. I believe that god is a craving, a need to be visible. So we scratch our signature into the earth like it was our school desk. As if we’re really learning something, as if we’re something worth teaching. You know what, if the earth knew any better, it’d rub us out of existence or bury us deep as the KT boundary.

I don’t know. I’m rambling, following the river. I think we may disagree about god? I hope that you still see me. I hope that you still tell me I’m home for you. And open the door despite the dead weight of me against it.

Maybe I’m too heavy. Maybe I’m sinking too fast. But I think you might save me from drowning. I think you could be a story that lasts.

Silence evacuates me.
Water pulls me under.
And you are a reaching hand.

My own guy

I believe everyone should feel hot as fuck at least once a year. I love weddings. The rituals, the whiskey, the dancing, the empty promises, the forced happy mindset, and the overall shallowness of it all. The dress code as well, fuck! I really enjoy wearing a suit. I see myself in the mirror getting ready and pumping up, and I can feel the moment where I exit my life and start being this guy, who knows everything about me and talks about my life using the third person. I can see that I'm different and maybe even better, cause I'm not there. All of the anxiety is gone, and he knows exactly how to talk to people and make them feel special. This guy is still awkward tho, but with the right joke and a compelling look, he can go from cringy to charming in no fucking time. He feels comfortable in plain sight, and his words become a riffle for constructed situations. He shoots jokes where they are needed, and the same goes for compliments, silence, or just a random anecdote. He's sweet as maple syrup, and people can't tell that he is not me, and for a few seconds, I also think I might be him. Until the mirror cracks, cause some idiot on his left told him that he looked like my brother in a selfie they just took, and then suddenly he's gone. I'm there instead, with all of my memories and resentment, with my holes and demons, trying to see if I can get that guy to come back, and I find him, waiting for me to leave inside a glass of scotch. He's the same, with the exception that now he craves validation. He needs to make sure the mirror won't break. Thus, he finds a group and tells a witty observation he's been crafting for the last hour, and the group loves him. Therefore I hate him. It's reciprocal (I think so at least), cause the next time it happens, he needs more alcohol to keep me away, and he's smart enough to know that it's impossible to keep up with that rhythm. Nonetheless, he believes in himself and pushes again with a shot of vodka, and this time he wins, cause he threw me out of the party, and I landed on my bed, inside the fucking next-day limbo, where for a few minutes we both exist in the same body. We stand up and get our phone, and we check our sweetheart's messages. I tell some of his stories, and he starts fading away thanks to my fear of her liking him too much. I scroll back to previous conversations, and he knows he's leaving soon. I find words of kindness and loving gestures, and inside them, I find a room where I'm the only one allowed, and first-person chat is mandatory.
